Welcome instructions

With this experiment we examine your performance on solving anagrams. An Anagram is a kind of word play. By rearranging a string of letters a meaningful word is produced, using all the original letters exactly once.
Here is an example: IONL -- LION
The initial string of letters is not necessarily a proper word or needs to have a meaning. Please do the following three examples to get acquainted with the task:

Welcome instructions2

Your task during this experiment will be to finish 9 pages with 4 anagrams on each page. You can only go to the next page if you have found the solution to all anagrams that are displayed on one screen. We will measure your performance by taking the time for each page individually. If you will not be able to solve all anagrams in a certain time, you will be able to go on to the next page without having found all anagrams. However, this will influence your overall performance rating. The total amount of time (each page added up) will determine your total performance. AHer the experiment you will get feedback on your performance
